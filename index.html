I managed a wrestling team.

Categories: Leadership

During my junior year of high school, I managed the academic magnet wrestling team, spending 76 hours on both practices and matches. My job was to ensure the team functioned well and matches went smoothly. During most events, there was one match on one mat at a time, and 2-3 schools were present. However, there were also invitationals, which meant more than ten teams and 3-4 mats had matches simultaneously. I was in charge of tracking our teams' results (and their future competitors' results for comparisons) on brackets and ensuring each member knew when it was almost their turn and when they should start warming up. In standard matches, I sat close to the mat with a scorebook to track each point earned and lost. In both invitationals and basic matches, I was often asked to video record certain matches, and it was important to show my support for each participant. During practices, my job was to keep track of everyone's weight, results, and specific points earned/lost from everyone's matches throughout the season, as well as help with drills and keeping time.

I've been playing violin for ten years.

Categories: Extracurricular, Leadership

I’ve played violin for ten years and participated in regional and All-State Orchestras since 2018. I came to the School of the Arts for violin in sixth grade and successfully auditioned for SOA’s High school Symphony orchestra, which I played in until I moved up to Sinfonietta Junior year. At the end of my Junior year, I traveled to Orlando, Florida, as a section leader of the second violins of the Sinfonietta ensemble. We participated in a National competition called the NOF (National Orchestra Festival) and won grand champions. I was a part of the District Orchestra from 2015 to 2018, where I became concertmaster ( the leading first violinist that all orchestra sections follow). From 2021 - 2023, I participated in a chamber music Charleston program(CMC), alternating between first and second violin parts. In March of 2022, as a member of the quintet, I participated in a masterclass conducted by the world-renowned Midori Goto. At the end of March 2022, my quintet participated in USC Chamber Music Day, where we received two individual two-hour-long coachings and a masterclass from USC staff and members of the Parker Quartet. I grew in a new way, mainly when I played my first violin, because of the soloistic part and the type of leadership it takes to keep the five-person ensemble together. Communication is unique when it comes to chamber music. In rehearsals, you must comment on everyones playing to improve the group’s sound, and this has to be done in a polite, direct, and effective manner so the players can understand your point quickly and incorporate it into their playing.

I participated in organizing a Halloween performance for kids.
